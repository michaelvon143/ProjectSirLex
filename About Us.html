PCNL HISTORY

The demand of a globalized era is so high that it requires a competitive labor force or the development of highly skilled and well-educated human resources. To attain these, all learning institutions must exert effort to harness the potentials of their students to have globally competitive graduates. Until a higher educational institution (HEI) was established after a survey was conducted by way of random sampling of parents from La Union and from the northern and western towns of Pangasinan whose children are in elementary and secondary schools could be prospective students of the proposed HEI.

After the organizational meeting of the incorporators, the organization came up with the name Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon (PCNL) with articles of incorporation and by-laws duly approved by the Securities and Exchange Commission, Baguio Extension Office on the 20th day of February 2007.

All CHED requirements were prepared by the group including the province’s prospective courses to be offered which are the Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA), Bachelor of Science in Accountancy (BSA), Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSE), Bachelor of Elementary Education (BEE), and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT). While waiting for the approval from the Commission on Higher Education (CHED), the Board of Trustees also discussed offering short-term courses by TESDA. After their technical visit, PCNL was permitted to offer Computer Hardware Servicing NC II, Computer Programming NC IV, PC Operation NC II, and Tourism Housekeeping NC II in SY 2007–2008.

In February 2008, CHED approved BSBA and BSA to start in SY 2008–2009. Later, BSE and BEE followed, then BSIT and Criminology. Teacher Education Programs aim to prepare educators; BSBA majors in Financial Management and HRDM aim to boost the economy; BSIT contributes to tech innovation; Criminology helps maintain peace; and TESDA courses enhance student skills and create college pathways.

PCNL is situated in a learning-friendly environment. Its “Old Building” was within Agoo Montessori Learning Center, while the “New Building” is beside Agoo Public Cemetery, surrounded by rice fields. Facilities include classrooms, Housekeeping and Dark Rooms, 2 Computer Labs, a Speech Lab, Science Lab, AV Room, library, faculty room, and an open court with a stage. The New Building houses more classrooms and the Administration Office. Based on the 5-year plan (2007–2012), facility improvements continue. Initial investment was ₱3.9M.

PCNL aims to be socially desirable and economically viable by increasing professionals in the region, enabling local and overseas employment, and contributing to industry growth, job generation, and better quality of life.

MISSION

In support of its philosophy, PCNL mandates itself to provide advanced quality instruction to its clientele in education,
the arts of humanities, the sciences, technology, business, and other professional and technical fields; promote
the advancement of knowledge through research; and help to improve the quality of community life through extension work.

VISION

To be a distinctive learning institution acknowledged far and wide for its innovative and flexible
approaches in working with its students, stakeholders, and the community to
create a more just and humane society and a more sustainable economy.
